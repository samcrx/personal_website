PhD Student
Zama & University of Rennes
October 2020 – Present
Paris & Rennes, France

My thesis focuses on TFHE, a recent fully homomorphic encryption scheme able to compute a bootstrapping in record time. We introduce an optimization framework to set the degrees of freedom inherent to homomorphic computations which gives non-experts the ability to use it (more) easily. We describe a plethora of new FHE algorithms which improve significantly the state of the art and limit, (if not remove) existing restrictions. Efficient open source implementations are already accessible. In particular, I am the scientific advisor for the Concrete Compiler project.
