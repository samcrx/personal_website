I am Samuel Tap, a dedicated researcher specializing in deep learning and homomorphic encryption. Currently, I am working toward a PhD at Zama, a pioneering company leading the advancements in Fully Homomorphic Encryption (FHE). FHE is a revolutionary encryption technique that allows computations on encrypted data without needing to decrypt it first. This ensures data privacy, even during processing, opening up numerous possibilities in secure cloud computing and privacy-preserving data analysis.

Before joining Zama, I worked at CryptoExpert where I delved into the intricacies of neural network layers tailored for specific encryption schemes. I also undertook an internship at Informatique Banque Populaire in Toulouse, focusing on bolstering the security measures of mobile applications.

On the academic front, I proudly hold an engineering degree from Ecole Centrale de Lille, majoring in data science and a M.S. in cryptography from the University of Versailles.
